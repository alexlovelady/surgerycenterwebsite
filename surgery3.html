Overview

Gastroparesis is a condition where the stomach's ability to empty its contents is delayed, causing nausea, vomiting, and bloating. Treatment options include dietary changes, medications, and, in severe cases, procedures like gastric electrical stimulation or surgery to facilitate stomach emptying.

Statistics

  • Gastroparesis affects approximately 5 million people in the United States.
  • Gastric electrical stimulation has a success rate of 60-70% in reducing symptoms in refractory cases.
  • Patients undergoing surgical interventions typically see improvements in quality of life and symptom management.

What to Expect

Pre-Operative Instructions

Before surgery, patients may undergo tests such as gastric emptying studies. Pre-operative preparation includes fasting and possibly adjusting medications that affect stomach motility.

The Procedure

The specific procedure depends on the treatment approach. Gastric electrical stimulation involves implanting a device that sends electrical pulses to the stomach muscles. Other surgical options may include pyloroplasty, which widens the opening between the stomach and small intestine.

Post-Operative Care

Post-operative care includes monitoring for complications such as infection or device malfunction. Dietary adjustments and follow-up appointments are essential for managing symptoms and ensuring the effectiveness of the treatment.

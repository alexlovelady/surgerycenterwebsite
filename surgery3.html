Overview

Breast augmentation is a cosmetic surgical procedure that involves the use of implants or fat transfer to increase the size of the breasts or restore breast volume lost after weight reduction or pregnancy. The procedure can also improve breast shape and symmetry.

Statistics

  • Breast augmentation is the most popular cosmetic surgical procedure in the United States, with over 300,000 procedures performed annually.
  • The procedure has a high satisfaction rate, with 98% of women reporting that their results met or exceeded their expectations.
  • Recovery typically takes 4 to 6 weeks, with most patients returning to normal activities within a week.

What to Expect

Pre-Operative Instructions

Prior to surgery, patients will undergo a consultation to discuss their goals, choose the appropriate type and size of implants, and review the surgical plan. It is important to stop smoking and avoid certain medications before surgery to reduce the risk of complications.

The Procedure

The surgery is typically performed under general anesthesia and takes about 1 to 2 hours. Incisions are made in inconspicuous areas to minimize visible scarring. The surgeon places the implants either under the chest muscle or directly behind the breast tissue, depending on the desired outcome.

Post-Operative Care

After surgery, patients are advised to wear a supportive surgical bra to help minimize swelling and support the healing breasts. Pain and discomfort are usually managed with prescribed medications. Follow-up visits will be scheduled to monitor recovery and ensure optimal results.

Overview

Chronic pain is a persistent condition that affects millions of people. Treatment options vary depending on the type and source of pain, and they may include medications, physical therapy, nerve blocks, and surgical interventions.

Statistics

  • Chronic pain affects over 50 million adults in the United States.
  • About 20 million people suffer from high-impact chronic pain that limits daily activities.
  • Multimodal pain management approaches have shown to improve outcomes significantly.

What to Expect

Pre-Treatment Instructions

Prior to initiating treatment, patients will undergo a comprehensive evaluation to determine the best course of action. This may include diagnostic imaging, lab tests, and consultations with pain management specialists.

The Procedure

Depending on the specific treatment, procedures may range from minimally invasive injections to more extensive surgical interventions. Nerve blocks, spinal cord stimulation, and intrathecal pain pumps are some of the advanced options available.

Post-Treatment Care

Post-treatment care is crucial for effective pain management. This may involve follow-up appointments, physical therapy, medication adjustments, and lifestyle modifications to help manage and reduce pain over time.

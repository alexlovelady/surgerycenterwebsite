Overview

Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG) is a type of heart surgery that improves blood flow to the heart. Surgeons use CABG to treat people with severe coronary artery disease (CAD), a condition in which a waxy substance called plaque builds up inside the coronary arteries, leading to reduced blood flow to the heart.

Statistics

  • Over 200,000 CABG surgeries are performed annually in the United States.
  • The procedure has a success rate of over 90%, with many patients experiencing significant improvements in symptoms and quality of life.
  • Recovery time can vary, but most patients return to normal activities within 6 to 12 weeks.

What to Expect

Pre-Operative Instructions

Patients will undergo pre-surgical testing, including blood tests, X-rays, and an electrocardiogram (EKG). It is important to follow the surgeonâ€™s instructions regarding medications and fasting before the procedure. Arrangements should be made for a lengthy recovery period at home.

The Procedure

The surgery is performed under general anesthesia and typically takes 3 to 6 hours. During CABG, a healthy artery or vein from the body is connected, or grafted, to the blocked coronary artery. The grafted artery or vein bypasses the blocked portion of the coronary artery, creating a new path for blood to flow to the heart.

Post-Operative Care

After surgery, patients will spend several days in the hospital for monitoring and recovery. Pain management, wound care, and physical activity will be closely managed. Cardiac rehabilitation may be recommended to help with recovery and to prevent future heart issues.
